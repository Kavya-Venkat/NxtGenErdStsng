Day 2 - Sri Sathya Sai Sthree Dharma Bodhini



The Erode Alumni of Anantapur campus, the Balvikas Alumni and Mahila Members of the Sri Sathya Sai Seva Organization of Erode District presented a skit based on Sri Sathya Sai Sthree Dharma Bodhini at the divine lotus feet of our beloved mother Sai on June 17, 2018 as a part of celebrations of the third pavitra parti yatra from Erode district June 15-17 2018.

1. Swami has manifested as Deha Maatha, physical mother and introduced us to this world.
2. For our sustenance, mother Sai assumed the form of Gomatha.
3. Swami held us on His lap as Bhoomatha while we were treading the earth’s surface.
4. As Veda Maatha, Swami blessed us with the codes of conduct to be followed.
5. As Desa Maatha, Swami instilled love for our motherland in us.
As Pancha Maatha, beloved Swami has sculpted our path towards Him.

A famous Tamil saying goes thus, “Maadharai Pirandhida Maathavam Seidhidal vendum”, meaning, “Tenacious penance is necessary to be born as women.”

The Sanskrit word “Sthree” refers to woman. It has three syllables – “Sa”, “Ta” and “Ra”
1. "Sa" signifies the Satvic qualities like forbearance, compassion, sacrifice and love.
2. "Ta" signifies the Tamo guna, which includes qualities like modesty and patience.
3. "Ra" signifies the Rajasic qualities such as courage and adventurous spirit.

In addition to other duties, Women have an exclusive sacred duty to restore Dharma in home which is of paramount importance given the current state of society with multitudes of distractions.

The very name “Sri Sathya Sai Sthree Dharma Bodhini” describes the sacred roles and responsibilities of women as told by our beloved Bhagawan. The three qualities in the term "Sthree" are to be found both in men and women. So this message is for all, as Bhagawan is the only Purusha and all others are Sthrees! Realising the God within is the end objective of every human.

It is rightly said that “God cannot be there always with everyone, so He sent His angels in disguise as Mothers.” The mother’s lap is the first school for every person. Only dedicated mothers can offer virtuous children to the nation. From ancient times, Indian scriptures have glorified the examples of great women like Maithreyi, Sita and Savithri whose lives remain as a source of inspiration for posterity. Women of today should make them as their role models and strive to be righteous always.

Being born as women is a great fortune and it is one’s primary responsibility to foster dharma in all phases of life. Mathru Moorthi Srimathi Eshwaramma serves as a shining example of the essence of Sri Sathya Sai Sthree Dharma Bodhini. Hail to Sri Sathya Sai Sthree Dharma Bodhini.
