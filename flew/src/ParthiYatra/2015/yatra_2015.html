Parthi Yatra - 2015

(August 7th - 8th - 9th)

Aum Sri Sairam, We the devotes of Erode District, Tamil Nadu are offering our loving Pranams at the divine lotus feet of our mother Sai. On the Eve of Bhagwans 90th Birthday celebrations, Swami has blessed us to undertake our Second Pavitra Parthi Yatra for a duration of three Days from August 7th to August 9th of the year 2015. It is worth mentioning here that, for the second consecutive time, our district is undertaking Parthi Yatra by a special charted train.

As a part of this Pavitra Parti Yatra, around 2300 Devotes took part reaching Prashanti Nilayam through the charted train from Erode offering their humble salutations to Swami. As stated earlier, tis is our second Parthi Yatra by a special Train. Incidentally these devotees include Students and staff members of Schools and Colleges, Business heads and others apart from Balavikas students, Balavikas Gurus, district youths, office beareres and members of Sai family. Most of them have not seen Swami in physical form and have expressed a strong desire to visit Prasanthi Nilayam where Swami is in his subtle and expanded form.

Needless to say, after reaching Prashanti Nilayam and having the darshan of our belowed Sai, they supremely happy and profusely contented. Further they have also expressed that they have experienced a peace and serenity here which is unique to Prasanthi Nilayam. In other words, according to them, Prasanthi Nilayam is beyond comparison.

It is to be noted that, as an offering to Bhagwan on the eve of 90th birthday celebrations, Erode district at present has 27 Samithis, 15 Bhajanamandalis and 300 Balvikas centres. The daily and periodical activities icludes Nagarsangeerthans, Narayanaseva, Balvikas classes and other regular activities. In connection with swamis 90th birthday celebrations we have undertaken 3 national projects with the blessings of Bhagwan.

  1. Supplying Sai Protein to the Expectent mothers and Children.
  2. Conducting Disaster Management Awareness Programme for the Schools, Colleges and also in rural areas.
  3. Running a Rural Vocational Training Centre.
