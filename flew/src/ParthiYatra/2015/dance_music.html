Dance & Musical Program - Parthi Yatra - 2015

(August 7th)

Swami has blessed the devotees of Erode district to undertake our Second consecutive Pavitra Parthi Yatra. The first day evening program at Sai Kulwant Hall started with a dance offering from the Balavikas children of the Sri Sathya Sai Matriculation School, Erode District. This was followed by a Musical Presentation by the Youth and Balavikas Children.



"Iraivan Sayi Thallikku Thangalin Erodu Anbu Kuzhanthaigalin Kruthagjnatha Poorvaka Paamalai" as an offering at the Lotus Feet on the 90th Birthday Year; Meaning thereby, “For the Sai Divine Mother, Thy Loving Erode children's Garland of Songs as A Grateful Offering".

This humble musical offering consists of devotional and classical songs sung by various Balvikas children and Sai Youths, currently studying in Schools and Colleges at Erode and its regions.

Notably, Shri R. Sai Prasad, has to his credit, that he is an alumnus of Sathya Sai Mirpuri College. He, as a little boy at the age of 10 sang , sitting in front of Swami. His grand parents are residents of Puttaparthi, serving in the Book Trust as Sai Sevaks and he is the son of our District President.

Master Mukund Swaminathan, a classical singer, studying in a Matriculation Hr Sec School, is cultural secretary of the school. He has been pursuing Carnatic music from the age of five, has participated in programmes at the age of seven itself in Jaya TV, ZEE Tamil, Sun TV, Vijay TV and SONY. He has won several awards. District First in Classical Music awarded by Govt. of Tamil Nadu. Dr.Balamurali Krishna Award in Ragamalika. He was among the top contestants in Airtel Super Singer Junior3 – Vijaya TV and Indian Idol Junior – Sony TV. He received "Nightingale Award" from Bharathiya Vidya Bhavan in the year 2014.

Kum. Sathya Sree and Sai Sree twin-sisters, who are learners of Carnatic Music, have special flair for the Divine Special ‘Rama Katha’. They are the siblings of the family dedicated to Sai Seva for the past 5 decades, whose late grand father had a credit to have sung Bhajans in front of Swami in the Mandir.

Kum. Sai Preethi, the other singer is learning Carnatic music from the age of 4. She has won many prizes at District level School competitions and has secured first prize in state level balvikas bhajan competition at the age of 12.

Kum. Ayswarya has attended youth camps conducted in Prasanthi Nilayam. She has learnt Carnatic Music, secured District level and State level prizes in various classical music and light music competitions. She has been named as "Voice of Kovai 2009"; conducted by Radio Mirchi.

Kum Ranjani and Madhuvanthi sisters have the credit of having sung before Swami once in Kodaikanal in the traditional style known in Tamil as “Paddhdhathi Bhajan”. Their late father has lead the Padhadhi Bhajan performed in Sai Kulwant Hall.

Once again we the Erode Kuzhanthaigal alikkum Kruthagjnatha Poorvaka Paamalai our Divine Mother SAI.
